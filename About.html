About

Priscilla Bourgoine has practiced as a clinical social worker for thirty-three years. Currently, she offers remote counseling with Ableto.com and teaches in the online School of Social Work as an adjunct professor at Simmons College. She earned a MSW from the University of Connecticut and an MFA in creative writing from Queens University of Charlotte. She is a nonfiction and fiction editor at R.KV.R.Y. Literary Magazine. Her work has appeared in Brain, Child; Grubwrites, New Hampshire Union Leader, and elsewhere. A graduate of GrubStreet’s Memoir Incubator program, for which she was awarded a scholarship, Priscilla is revising her first memoir: REMEMBER TO BREATHE: A Mother’s First Year of Learning to Live with a Broken Heart. She has been awarded writing residencies at: Virginia Center for the Creative Arts, Turkey Land Cove Foundation, and Ragdale. She lives in southern New Hampshire with her husband.
